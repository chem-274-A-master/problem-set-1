Background: Diatomic Molecules - The Harmonic Oscillator

The behavior of molecular bonds is frequently modeled using a harmonic oscillator, a classical system also known as a "mass on a spring." This system is governed by Hooke's Law, which states that the force exerted by the spring, F(x)F(x)F(x), is directly proportional to the displacement xxx from its equilibrium position and proportional to the spring constant kkk:

F(x)=−kxF(x) = -kxF(x)=−kx

The potential energy U(x)U(x)U(x) of a harmonic oscillator is given by:

U(x)=12kx2U(x) = \frac{1}{2} kx^2U(x)=21​kx2

In the context of chemistry, the simplest molecule with a bond is the diatomic molecule, which consists of two atoms. We can model this diatomic molecule as two masses connected by a spring, as described by the equations above.
